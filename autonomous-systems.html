Autonomous Systems Architecture

European Patent EP26025008.9

The standard industry architecture for autonomous drone and UAS systems creates a structural latency problem that cannot be resolved at the code level. When Python-based AI target recognition and computer vision systems communicate with C++ hardware actuation layers, the translation overhead between those two environments introduces timing delays that compromise flight stability, targeting precision, and autonomous decision-making at the speeds required for effective operation. This is an architectural problem, not a software one.

European Patent EP26025008.9 covers a proprietary architectural framework that eliminates that translation bottleneck at the design level. The framework resolves the latency conflict between AI guidance systems, visual processing systems, and hardware actuation by restructuring the logical architecture through which those layers communicate, drawing on principles demonstrated in advanced driverless vehicle systems and applying them to the specific constraints of autonomous flight control.

What drone manufacturers and UAS engineering companies receive through a licensing arrangement is not a product to integrate into an existing stack. It is a Technical Data Package: a legally protected, architecturally complete blueprint that the licensee's own engineering team implements in-house, within their own sovereign environment, under their own security protocols and classification requirements. There is no external vendor dependency. There is no foreign code in the production system. There is no ongoing licence that can be revoked or renegotiated. The licensee takes ownership of the architecture permanently.

This model is designed specifically for European drone manufacturers, sovereign defence programmes, and engineering companies for whom supply chain independence and technical sovereignty are not optional requirements but contractual and strategic imperatives.
