The Estate Infrastructure Opportunity

Large commercial estates, distribution centres, car parks, and logistics hubs now qualify as de facto energy infrastructure under recent UK planning reform. The Class OA permitted development rights introduced in December 2023 allow solar canopies on non-domestic off-street parking subject to prior approval, materially improving planning viability for large retail and logistics estates. Most property teams have not yet fully absorbed what this means for asset value and operating cost.

The UK grid faces a material capacity reduction as EDF's remaining Advanced Gas-Cooled Reactor stations close. Hartlepool and Heysham 1 are scheduled to cease generation in March 2028. Heysham 2 and Torness are expected to follow in March 2030. Operators with significant electricity demand and the physical footprint to generate, store, and flex their own supply have a closing window to act ahead of that constraint.

The businesses best placed to capture this opportunity are those that already own or control their logistics and distribution infrastructure, carry large and predictable electricity loads across food retail, refrigeration, or manufacturing operations, and have the estate scale to justify a serious review of on-site generation, battery storage, and demand flexibility as a revenue and resilience strategy rather than a symbolic sustainability gesture.

How an Engagement Works

Commercial Architecture Review

A tightly scoped diagnostic of selected assets, deliverable within weeks. We establish what the estate could realistically generate, store, and flex, and what that is worth in revenue, resilience, and carbon reduction terms. This is the fixed-fee entry point for every engagement.

Structuring and Negotiation

Once the opportunity is defined, we structure the commercial arrangements required to act on it. This includes Power Purchase Agreements, grid export arrangements, joint venture frameworks, and demand flexibility contracts that make the project credible to your board and to any external capital partners.
