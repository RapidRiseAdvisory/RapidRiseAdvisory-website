Frequently Asked Questions

Working With Us

What types of organisations does RapidRise Advisory work with?

We work across two distinct segments.

For large established organisations, including major retailers, logistics operators, REITs, and commercial estate owners, we advise on how physical infrastructure, particularly energy generation and storage, can be structured to protect operational performance and create long-term commercial value.

For earlier-stage technology ventures with a physical, industrial, or sovereign component, we provide commercial architecture and revenue strategy to accelerate institutional scaling and capital formation.

I run a large business. How is this relevant to us?

If your organisation operates a substantial physical estate, whether fulfilment centres, distribution hubs, retail stores, car parks, or logistics networks, there is a growing structural question about whether parts of that estate should now be treated as energy infrastructure rather than passive overhead.

We help you answer that question with commercial rigour, screening your sites, identifying where on-site generation and storage carry operational value, and structuring the commercial and planning pathway to act on it.

We already purchase renewable electricity. Why would we need this?

Renewable procurement is a tariff arrangement. The power still arrives through the same local grid connections, subject to the same capacity constraints and peak import costs. As electrification accelerates across your operations, whether through fleet charging, automated processing, or expanded operating hours, the question shifts from energy cost to energy resilience.

We help identify the specific sites where behind-the-meter generation and storage can protect throughput, charger uptime, and service continuity in ways that procurement alone cannot.

I am an early-stage technology company. Do you work with startups?

Yes, but selectively. We focus on ventures at Technology Readiness Levels 7 to 9, meaning the product has moved beyond the lab and is demonstrating pilot capability or early commercial deployment.

We specialise in physical, industrial, and sovereign technology domains such as energy, hardware, defence, critical infrastructure, and strategic supply chains.

Pure software and SaaS ventures are handled through our sister brand, RapidRise.io.

How Engagements Work

What does a typical engagement look like for a large organisation?

Every engagement begins with a Commercial Architecture Review, a fixed-fee diagnostic that screens your estate, identifies priority sites, and produces a board-ready assessment of where on-site energy infrastructure carries genuine operational and commercial value.

The diagnostic typically takes two to four weeks. If the findings justify it, we then work with you on planning, commercial structuring, and delivery strategy, drawing on a specialist bench of grid connection, solar and storage design, and energy procurement experts.

What does a typical engagement look like for an earlier-stage venture?

Most engagements begin with a short diagnostic followed by a focused work plan covering commercial contracts, capital strategy, and stakeholder mapping. We work hands-on with founders and leadership teams to progress bankable outcomes, not just presentations.

What is a Commercial Architecture Review?

It is our entry-point diagnostic for large organisations. We screen selected assets across your estate to establish the realistic generation, storage, and flexibility potential of each site, the planning route available under current permitted development rights, the revenue and resilience value of acting, and the commercial structure required to proceed.

The deliverable is a prioritised site assessment with recommended next steps, designed to support internal decision-making at board level.

Do you offer ongoing advisory beyond the diagnostic?

Yes. Where the diagnostic reveals material value, we offer ongoing advisory support through Fractional Chief Revenue Officer or Fractional Chief Commercial Officer arrangements, providing sustained strategic and commercial leadership without the overhead of a permanent hire.

We also manage specialist delivery partners for grid connection assessment, system design, and commercial structuring on your behalf.

Sectors and Expertise

Which sectors do you work in?

For large organisations: major retailers, grocery chains, logistics and fulfilment operators, commercial real estate investment trusts, and any business operating a substantial UK physical estate with exposure to energy cost, grid constraint, or fleet electrification.

For earlier-stage ventures: energy, hardware, defence, critical infrastructure, autonomous systems, and strategic supply chains.

Do you work with property companies and REITs?

Yes. We work with listed and private property owners to assess whether parts of their estate, particularly retail parks, distribution centres, and assets with large surface car parks, carry strategic infrastructure value that conventional property classification does not capture. The lens is energy generation, battery storage, tenant energy resilience, and the impact on asset valuation and rental income durability.

Do you install solar panels or battery storage?

No. We are a strategic advisory practice, not an installer or EPC contractor.

We provide the commercial architecture, site screening, planning pathway, and commercial structuring that determines whether and where installation makes sense.

Where a project proceeds to deployment, we can introduce you to vetted specialist partners for design, engineering, and installation.
