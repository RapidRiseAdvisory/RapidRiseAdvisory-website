About Daniel Lee Knight

RapidRise Advisory was founded by Daniel Lee Knight to solve a specific commercial problem: large organisations sitting on infrastructure value they have not yet priced, and early-stage technology companies building products they cannot yet sell at scale.

Daniel brings over 25 years of experience selling complex analytical platforms and intelligence infrastructure to institutional buyers across capital markets, energy, maritime, and defence. This includes seven years at S&P Global and IHS Markit, where he was responsible for USD 42 million in maritime intelligence and defence sales to sovereign and institutional clients. Earlier roles include Debtwire, Mergermarket, and Ruesch International.

That background means he understands how large operators and institutional investors make capital allocation decisions, how property and operations teams assess strategic risk, and how to structure a commercial thesis that lands with a CFO, an estates board, or a sovereign procurement office.

Estate Infrastructure Advisory

RapidRise Advisory’s primary practice works with major UK retailers, logistics operators, petrol forecourt networks, property REITs, and institutional landowners to assess whether parts of their physical estate carry strategic infrastructure value that conventional property classification does not capture. The lens is on-site energy generation, battery storage, demand flexibility, and the commercial structuring required to act on it.

The UK grid faces a material capacity reduction as EDF’s remaining Advanced Gas-Cooled Reactor stations close over the next four years. Operators with significant electricity demand and the physical footprint to generate, store, and flex their own supply have a closing window to act ahead of that constraint. RapidRise Advisory provides the commercial architecture, site screening, and structuring that connects that physical opportunity to board-level decision-making and institutional capital.

Autonomous Systems and Deep Tech

RapidRise Advisory also operates two specialist practices: an autonomous systems architecture licensing programme under European Patent EP26025008.9, serving European drone manufacturers and sovereign defence programmes, and a deep tech commercialisation practice for founders of critical infrastructure and hard technology ventures at Technology Readiness Levels 7 to 9.
